How it started.

Four teenage friends spent their afternoons at the local skate park, bonding over skateboarding and music. They all shared a love for rock and punk music and would often play together during breaks from skating. Over time, they realized they had a strong connection and began talking about starting a band.


They decided to name their band Stage Fright, inspired by their shared fear of performing in front of others. Each of them had their own role in the band: Landon played guitar, Spencer was on drums, Connor handled the bass, and David was the lead singer. They started practicing regularly, writing songs about their lives and experiences. What started as a fun hobby quickly became a serious project, and soon they were preparing for their first live show at a local venue.

What we sound like.

The Fall of Autumn

Failure to Cope

Vexed

Our three albums take you through a range of emotions, each from a different perspective, leaving you shocked and amazed. The Fall of Autumn leans more towards melodic rock, offering a smoother sound compared to our more aggressive work. That’s where Vexed comes in—this album is for those craving intensity and the extreme. If you’re looking for that next level, Vexed will make you want to jump up and down while screaming along. Meanwhile, our most recent album, Failure to Cope, is calmer and more relaxed than the other two. However, this album will still take you on an emotional rollercoaster, challenging your emotions in a way that only music can.
